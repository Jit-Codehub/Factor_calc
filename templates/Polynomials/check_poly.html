Polynomial Rules: What Makes Up Polynomial

Polynomial is a mix of terms isolated with the signs called + or - and also for quick understanding of how to find it easily, we have come up with the authentic rules to identify and know what polynomials include:

  • Polynomials contain variables, constants, exponents, and arithmetic operations (except division by variable).
  • The letters such as x, y, z, a, and b are called variables.
  • The constants are like whole numbers, for example, 3, 5, 11. These constants are placed with variables and also on their own in the polynomial expressions.
  • When it comes to Exponents there are generally linked to variables, and also with a constant. Examples of exponents in polynomials are 6² or x³.
  • Arithmetic operations play a vital role in forming a polynomial expression. For instance, multiplication is like 2x, multiplication & addition is like 6x+5, and subtraction is written as x-5.

Polynomial Rules: What ISN'T a Polynomial

  • Variables in fraction form.
  • Variables in the denominator don't make it as a polynomial expression, similar negative exponents as a-b = 1/ab
  • Other functions such as trigonometric, absolute values, logarithms, etc., and non-whole powers (roots) make not a polynomial expression.
  • Variable in exponents, for instance exp(x) = ex
