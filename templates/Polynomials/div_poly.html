What is the Polynomial Long Division?

A polynomial is an algebraic expression with several terms. The elements of a polynomial are constants, variables, coefficients, arithmetic operators and exponents. Generally, the long division method is used to divide the polynomials. It follows dividing a polynomial by another polynomial having the same or lower degree. The other method used to find division of polynomials is synthetic division method.

How to Divide Polynomials Using the Long Division Method?

The below-included are the steps to compute the two polynomials division with long division method. Use these techniques to check the result in a fraction of seconds.

  1. Firstly, arrange polynomials in the descending order of their degrees.
  2. Write dividend in between ")(" and divisor before ")".
  3. Start performing a long division method.
  4. Divide the first term of the dividend by the first term of the divisor. The obtained result is the first term of the quotient.
  5. And multiply the result by the divisor. Write the product below the dividend.
  6. Subtract the product from the dividend to get a new polynomial.
  7. Repeat steps 4 to 6 with the generated new polynomials till there are no more terms left and the remainder should be 0.
